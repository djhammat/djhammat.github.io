                                     John D. Rockefeller Synopsis

           John D. Rockefeller was the most successful person in the world.  He used good and bad tactics to take out competitors. John D. Rockefeller was born on July-8-1839 at Richford, New York. His Father John D. Rockefeller Senior was a doctor who claimed that he knew how to cure cancer. His father was accused of rape and his family moved to Cleveland Ohio (Poole 1). John D. Rockefeller was raised there. His mother Eliza Davison was a religious and disciplined women. John was second oldest out of the five siblings. Rockefeller has one son, his name is John D Rockefeller Jr. John has 4 daughters Edith Rockefeller McCormick, Alta Rockefeller Prentice, Elizabeth Rockefeller Strong, and Alice Rockefeller, Total of five children. This family was poor until John became an entrepreneur. He lived in the Industrialization era which caused  John to be motivated in business and advancement. John became extremely wealthy due to refining oil. His company name was called the Standard Oil Company. Rockefeller used many tactics to become head of the industry. He saved money by manufacturing his own barrels, building his own warehouse and buying his own pipeline (Brown 2). Many people referred him as a captain of industry. But there’s a catch. According to the (History.com) staff, the catch was John sold gas at cheap prices which was not benefiting competitors which caused competitors to sell their businesses. They also refered him as a Robber Baron. Congress passed law’s like the Sherman Antitrust act which helped competitors. Later he gave his business to his family. Soon he died at the age of 97 at Ormond Beach Florida.
